The average person loses hundreds of euros every year to subscriptions they've forgotten about — streaming services, free trials that renewed, apps no longer used. Your Financial Copilot finds these for you automatically.

## How Fiverse identifies unknown subscriptions

During your initial sync and every time your accounts refresh, Fiverse scans your transactions for recurring charges. It flags anything that looks like a subscription — even small, easy-to-miss ones — and groups them for your review.

You'll find these under the **AI Insights** section on your Home screen. A typical insight might read:

> *"You could save €47/month by cancelling unused subscriptions."*

## What to do with a flagged subscription

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review the list">
    Tap the insight to see a full list of detected recurring charges — name, amount, and billing frequency.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Keep or cancel">
    Decide for each one whether it's worth keeping. For anything you want to cancel, the app guides you on next steps.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track going forward">
    Once you confirm a subscription, Fiverse monitors it as an expected recurring expense and alerts you if the amount changes unexpectedly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Fiverse cannot cancel subscriptions on your behalf. It will show you exactly what you're being charged and when, so you can take action directly with the provider.
</Note>
